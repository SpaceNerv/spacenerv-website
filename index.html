What it is

Every tap is a photograph
taken in darkness.

Passive chips embedded in architectural surfaces, materials, and objects become phygital sensing nodes. A phone wakes the chip — the building speaks through the gesture. The tap is simultaneously the physical operational act and its authenticated digital record.

No dedicated hardware. No app. The tap is the atom of the entire architecture — the indivisible moment when a phone meets a chip and the material begins to remember. Intelligence emerges, slowly, from the aggregate.

The Tap
A single authenticated gesture. Phone to surface. The physical action and its digital record are one.

The hardware

Three instruments.
One gesture of light.

Every deployment rests on one of three chip substrates, chosen for the material, the context, and the weight of the memory being kept. All three answer the same gesture — a phone meeting a chip in the dark.

01
NERV·ROUTE
The torch. A quiet chip embedded in any surface. Passive, universal, patient. Lights a path to the memory already stored.
02
NERV·ACTIVE
The flashbulb. Passively powered, actively confirming. A small light, a soft tone, a moment of certainty. For gestures that must be remembered exactly.
03
NERV·SENSE
The magnifier. A chip that reads the room while it waits. Temperature, motion, material fatigue. The surface becomes a sensor that has been listening all along.
The octave

Intelligence emerges
at harmonic thresholds.

The plexus does not scale linearly. It climbs in octaves — each threshold a new class of self-knowledge becoming possible. Contact, condition, rhythm, anomaly, pattern, biography, typology. Each level requires the previous to be stable before the next can emerge.

One hand and one chip is the first octave. Multiple deployments speaking across a city is the fifth. A civilisation reading its own spatial biography is the seventh. Beyond that — the Grove — the planetary plexus of human spatial cognition.

The interface

A web app that appears
at the moment of contact.

The moment a phone meets a chip, a context-aware web app opens in the browser. No install. No account. No download. The interface is summoned by the gesture itself — and disappears when the work is done.

01
Context-aware forms
The form that opens is the form this node needs. A kitchen hood asks kitchen questions. A fire extinguisher asks compliance questions. The node decides.
02
Offline-first
The app works without signal. Taps are recorded locally, then sync when the network returns. Nothing is lost in the basement or the stairwell.
03
Operator sovereignty
Every operator sees their own tap history. No one else does. The Concordat ensures the data belongs to the hand that made it.

The phone is the instrument. The chip is the string. The web app is the note that sounds when they meet — and the silence that follows when they part. Nothing to install. Nothing to remember. Nothing to maintain.
